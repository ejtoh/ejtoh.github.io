Hello! I’m Elaine — a third year majoring in Computer Science (Games) with a minor in Technical Game Art at the University of Souther California (USC). As you can assume, I love games, and I’ve extended my enjoyment for playing and watching games to making them! I am largely interested in video game programming and design, but I’m also hoping to add on some 3D game art and UI/UX to my skill set! Additionally, besides my game-centric pursuits, I am interested in media and diversity and accessibility in the tech industry.


Throughout my time at USC, I’ve worked as a Teaching Assistant for the Video Game Programming (ITP 380) course for just a little over a year now! Working as a TA has helped me to process and understand other people’s code, and ultimately work to explain concepts as clearly and succinctly as possible. Having taken this class earlier, I quickly learned how to program games, but being a TA allowed me to fully understand the concepts I had previously learned. I also used to be journalism, and worked as reporter and editor back in high school for years — so I enjoy writing a lot and have become super detail-oriented from my expereince.


I hope this website has seveed as a small introduction into what I do, what I love, and what I want to keep pursing and growing towards!
